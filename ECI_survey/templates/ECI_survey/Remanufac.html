Before we continue, we want to give you some basic information about the nature of remanufactured products.

A remanufactured/refurbished product is a product that has been returned to the manufacturer for a variety of reasons. For example, a consumer might find a defect requiring return of the product, or the consumer might simply change his/her mind about owning the product. In other cases, retailers simply return unsold products to the manufacturer. Whatever the reason for the return, the manufacturer must remanufacture the product by testing and ensuring that the product is still fully functional. After a product is refurbished by the original equipment manufacturer, the manufacturer offers the product for sale as a "remanufactured" product. The remanufactured product often carries the same warranty as a new product and the warranty is offered by the original equipment manufacturer.
